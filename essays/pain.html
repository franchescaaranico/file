embracing the pain

October 18, 2024

Lately, I’ve been haunted by a thought that feels both comforting and slightly twisted, that no matter how many times love (platonic, familial, romantic) has disappointed me, I never really give up on it. Despite every emotional whiplash, every heartbreak that left me gasping for clarity, I still believe in love, perhaps more than I should. You’d think that after all the mess, I’d learn to protect myself better. But instead, I’ve made it through without losing faith on it.

It’s not that I’m grateful for the pain, exactly. It’s more that I’ve learned to see meaning in it. The highs and lows are all maddening, but it also feels visceral in a way that nothing else does. Love’s turbulence reminds me that I am alive. The ache that followed was the weight of having cared. And pain, after all, doesn’t last forever. It comes and goes like weather, but while it’s here, it colors everything vividly. And without it, joy would not make sense.

Right now, I’m in that familiar ache again. But truth be told, a huge part of me does not hate it, not masochistically, but more of anticipatorily. Tricking myself into believing I had everything under my control by voluntarily staying for the final scene, despite knowing the ending that’s coming. A descent in its most melancholic form. And yet, beneath that romanticized layer lies something bleaker, that I’m often the first to wound myself. I’ve learned the strange comfort of self-inflicted pain, through self-doubt, self-rejection, self-blame, and self-withdrawal. Somehow, it feels safer that way. If I break myself first, no one else gets to. A kind of self-preservation disguised as self-sabotage. Through it, I’ve learned my limits, the boundaries of how much pain I can endure.

I’m not sure if anyone else would understand this logic, or if it even qualifies as logic. Maybe it’s just the way I’ve made sense of my own tenderness. Maybe it’s how I cope with the risk of being an emotionally expressive human. Because to love, to really love, is to gamble with pain and I’d rather lose than not play at all.
