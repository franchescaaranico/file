bidding farewell to online friends

August 14, 2025

I have this habit of making my exits a bit conspicuous. I can’t seem to disappear without leaving something behind. A letter. A handmade gift. A message filled with too many thank-yous and too many sorrys. My goodbyes always come wrapped in ribbons and reverence, as if I am archiving people instead of letting them go.

Maybe it started when I realized that most relationships, especially the online ones, don’t have clear endings. People just drift away. Conversations stop. Usernames change. One day, someone who once felt like home becomes just another silent bubble on your screen. So instead of waiting for the drift, I started making my exits intentional. If I was going to leave, I wanted to honor the part of me that stayed.

The farewells are not just for the people I am leaving behind, but also for the version of myself that existed with them. Every friendship creates its own small universe. When it ends, that universe disappears too. The goodbye becomes less about closure and more about preservation, a final attempt to hold something still before time carries it away.

I think I have always been afraid of becoming forgettable. Not in the grand sense of being remembered forever, but of vanishing without acknowledgment. I wanted the people who mattered to know that they mattered. I wanted there to be evidence that, for a brief moment, our lives touched and changed one another.
