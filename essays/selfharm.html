why self-harm comforts you

April 11, 2023

You are out with your friends, laughing loudly like the happy person everyone believes you to be. Your laughter rings clear and convincing, a perfect disguise for the mind that never stops screaming its rebellion against existence. No one would ever suspect that while you joke about the smallest things, another part of you is carefully calculating an escape route from life itself. You have mastered the art of composure because no one has ever seen you defeated by challenges. The world rewards strength, and you have learned to imitate it well.

It is satisfying to appear in control with your life. The thrill in managing the madness into neat compartments that conceal the storm. Yet, beneath that structure lies the unbearable truth, control is an illusion. When life becomes a hurricane, the only thing that seems to be in your control is your own death.

You return home exhausted. You sit at your desk, trying to complete your duties and errands, but your thoughts scatter like frightened birds. The noise inside your head grows louder until language collapses. You ache so deeply that it numbs you. You write reminders on sticky notes for things you need to do, promising yourself you’ll get to them later. But days blur into nights, and weeks later, those notes become memorials of things you failed to do.

You wake up at midnight, walk to the kitchen and grab a bread knife. The cold metal presses against your skin, you feel the blood trickling down your arms, and for once, the pain translates into something visible. The sight of blood gets you excited as your body releases adrenaline, giving a brief moment of bliss. You tend to the wound with care, almost tenderness. You give yourself the comfort you always wished others had given you.

You wade into the ocean with the intention of never resurfacing. The waves seem gentle enough to erase you. Yet as the water rises to your neck, something in you stirs. You realize it isn’t death you long for. It's the compound of feelings that you experienced as you injure and heal yourself. Self-harm calmed you.
