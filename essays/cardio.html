how i lost 13 pounds in 3 months

latest stats: [age 24], [height 161 cm], [weight 57.5 kg], [BMI 22.2]
latest BWH measurements: 35–28–38

October 28, 2021

intro
Back in my teens, I never really cared much about my weight as long as I could still do sports, run fast, and play tag with friends. But when I was 18, during PE class, we had our BMI measured, and mine was 24.7. A healthy BMI ranges from 18.5 to 24.9, while 25 to 29.9 is considered overweight, which meant I was close to being classified as overweight. So, I became determined to lose weight and bring my BMI down to around 22. While part of my motivation came from insecurity and social pressure, a bigger part came from becoming aware of the health metrics and wanting to become a healthier version of myself.

I implemented a 30-day workout routine, but by the end of the month, I eventually continued doing it out of habit, and after the third month, I had lost 13 pounds. What was fascinating to me was how uneven the weight reduction was in my body. While my arms and waist got slimmer, and my chest and stomach got flatter, my thighs and butt still stayed a bit bigger. I thought I was doing my exercise wrong, but I realized later that it was actually conventionally a good thing to have thicker butt and thighs.

my daily 30-minute workout
Stretching (3–5 minutes) Before anything, I stretch my whole body. Head tilts, slow neck rolls, shoulder rolls, I reach my arms up, bend side to side, and stretch my legs a bit, etc. etc., warming up every part of my body from top to bottom.
Stand twist (30 reps) I stand with my feet shoulder-width apart and twist my upper body left to right while keeping my hips steady.
Stand cross crunch (20 reps each side) I bring my elbow down to meet the opposite knee, switching sides every rep.
High punch (40 reps) I throw punches upward like I’m trying to hit the ceiling, one arm at a time. I usually do this while sitting in the air.
Stand full crunch (20 reps) With both hands behind my head, I bring my elbow down toward the knee on the same side. Then I return to standing, switch sides, and repeat on each side.
Torso twist (30 reps) I keep my arms up and twist side to side, really squeezing my core.
Stand crunch stretch legs (20 reps each side) ...i forgot how it looks like xD
Side crunch (20 reps each side) Another type of crunch, but this time I do it on the floor.
Curl ups (50 reps) I lie down, knees bent, and lift my upper body halfway up. Not fully sitting, just enough to make my abs hurt.
Crunch (100 reps) A smaller, tighter version of curl ups. I focus on squeezing my stomach every time I go up.
Squats (50 reps) Feet apart, I lower myself down, then push back up. I like it when it burns my thighs.
Jumping jacks (50 reps) The classic. Jump feet out, hands up, then back in.
Jogs and high knees (5 minutes each) I jog in place first, then switch to lifting my knees high.
Squat on wall (1 to 2 minutes) I lean my back against the wall and slide down like I’m sitting on an invisible chair.
Tricep dips (20 reps) I use a chair or the edge of my bed, lower myself down with my arms, then push back up.
Front lunges (15 reps each leg) I step one foot forward, bend both knees, then push back up and switch.
Front planks (1 minute) I hold myself up on my elbows and toes, body straight like a board.
Left planks (1 minute) I turn to my left side and balance on one elbow, holding my body up.
Right planks (1 minute) Same thing on the right side.
Walk (5–10 minutes) At the end, I walk it off to cool down and catch my breath.

after 3 months
I quit my strict workout routine because I was satisfied with how I felt in my body. A lot had changed after 3 months. I walked more confidently, I felt sexier, and I could try out and fit into all the cute outfits that I wasn’t able to before. I started wearing crop tops and not looking like Pooh. But perhaps my body was so used to exercising that it craved it a lot. So I still exercise, but not as intense as before. I exercise 15 minutes every other day, and my routine isn’t that specific anymore. Usually, it’s just me dancing silly until I sweat and running laps. I bought a hula hoop and a jumping rope, and I incorporate those into my 15-minute regular exercise. It was fun!

eating Less
Food played a big part too. During my fitness journey, I had to cut down on how much I ate. I love food. Almost every kind of food is my favorite, even the ones I’m allergic to. I’d rather take antihistamines than not eat them at all. So this was probably one of the hardest parts. Back then, I used to eat around 3 cups of rice in every meal, but I cut it down to just 1 cup. I also stopped snacking whenever I felt like it. Before, I’d snack almost every hour without even thinking about it. Now, I only snack at least twice a day and try to be more mindful of when I’m actually hungry. After I lost weight, I noticed I didn’t crave as much food anymore. I got full faster, and my body adjusted to eating smaller portions compared to before.
