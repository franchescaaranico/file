don’t yell at me, or i’ll cry on my birthday

March 28, 2026

What fascinates me about people is how their attitude changes when deadlines approach or money is involved. They get cranky and throw adult tantrums. On my birthday, someone had loans to pay and got ten minutes left before the cutoff or else the interest would double. She asked for my help. The person I knew, usually composed, transformed into an erratic, panicking child, screaming in my face. I completed the necessary bank transactions in under five minutes and told her respectfully that shouting at me wouldn’t solve a thing.

Working with people who become grumpy during crises is not new to me. I used to work with clients who submit documents at the last minute and expect me to sort out their finances on time. Who am I, DC’s Flash? Can you please stop shouting at me? I may look composed with an unfazed gaze, but when you yell, it feels like you’ve shoved my heart into a blender and turned it into a smoothie. Saying you broke it into pieces would be an understatement, I’m actually a softie. I can only sit with my emotions during my late-night long bath, when my tears camouflage with the shower water for half an hour.

It was my birthday yesterday. I spent it working and planning my study schedule for the next nine months. Apparently last February, right after finding out I passed my degree, I immediately enrolled in a review class, with no second thoughts. I haven’t told a soul about the specifics yet, and I can’t even bring myself to say it out loud to myself or write about it here. I feel as though the thing between me and this premature dream is very intimate. I don't want people to have something to say about it and get in between me and this new rabbit I am chasing.

I did eat cake though while facing my computer. I received two flower bouquets, and a few birthday emails and chats from friends. I sank into my pillows for half the day, replacing my thoughts with the songs playing in the background. Am I sad? Not really. Tired maybe. I’m content with my life right now. I have no energy to socialize, so I went on a few solo dates this week for the sake of needing to celebrate my birthday and graduation, just wandering the city, trying foods I’d never tried before. It was a fun one-person party that felt like three. If I can entertain a crowd I can obviously entertain myself, so cheers to that. Happy 24th birthday!

I have two birthday wishes, neither material. First, I hope my sickening, ceaseless ambition and drive for status that I’m so in denial about never push out the warmth in my heart. Second, if I achieve the big thing I want this year, I hope I handle it with more humility. If I fail, I hope I can accept it gracefully and bounce back to try again.

There is also a silly yet comforting recurring dream I indulge in at night, where a future king on his noble horse from a distant universe appears right in front of my door and tells me I’m a long-lost royalty. He has come to save me from this life. Then I go with him and become his queen. I'll be caught up in a political conspiracy and immediately executed by guillotine. Death is peace. Death is my ultimate subconscious dream.
