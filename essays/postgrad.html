post‑grad existential crisis and loneliness

April 27, 2026

Life has been great recently. It feels so good to be out of college and finally do whatever I want. The good news is that I am responsible for my own life. The bad news is that I am responsible for my own life, both terrifying and empowering. I went to a few parties halfway through April. I had a conversation with some friends, and they confessed they were secretly in relationships behind each other’s backs. It was a plot twist coming from the same mouths that once said they didn’t need romance and didn’t want kids in this economy. Now they’ve started talking about how life is happier with a partner or companion. While I was happily listening to their love lives and finding out that other people I know are getting engaged, buying houses, or already have kids, I suddenly felt fomo.

I have always felt complete and happy on my own, but hearing those romantic experiences made me yearn for someone. Suddenly, I longed for a reality different from the one I was living and felt a pang as I realized I might always fall short of it. It felt impossible and unimaginable to have what they have because I genuinely do not know what I want or whether I even want it.

Last year I tried dating and genuinely put in effort to connect with the people I was seeing. I wanted to feel the same affection and excitement they seemed to have for me. But I couldn't feel my heartbeat, there were no butterflies in my stomach. I just felt empty and numb. I realized that maybe romance isn't for me... no matter how much I push that genre into my life it feels... out of place?

I feel like two beliefs I have are to blame. One is devoting your life to your art and pouring all your love into it will silence the craving for romance. Two is the moment you start seeking male validation that is when you start abandoning yourself. Those beliefs have kept me from having delusions and not giving weight to men's opinions about me. There was a time a guy told me I was ugly and boring. I expected it to hurt more than it did... it stung, of course, but it didn’t really shake my self-image because I’m aware of my strengths. On the other hand, when someone calls me pretty, it also doesn’t get in my head, because I’m just as aware of my insecurities.

But I am still human. I still crave connection, whatever form it may take. Recently, I had one of those rare nights when I felt lonely, perhaps triggered by the conversation I had with my friends. The only ways I know how to deal with loneliness are getting drunk or ████████████ the emptiness away. But someone said I needed therapy. A therapy?

God forbid a human being longing for connection so badly it’s considered a mental illness. Look at this pathetic woman who is an attention whore that seeks genuine connection, a social animal hardwired to seek support across generations for survival, a normal human with a healthy hypothalamus that treats sexual urges as a quick dopamine snack that shortcuts feeling good when life feels heavy. How humiliating is it to be like her? a normal human having normal human emotions, yuck. But seriously, 90% of the time, I feel happy and content of my life. It's just that I'm also okay feeling sad too. I like to embrace both my good and bad emotions, and allow them to flow through me.

Why is ██████████ ██████████ so hard, and why do I actually like doing hard things? I must be crazy. I must be a masochist. Why am I willingly putting myself in complicated situations when I can live a normal, comfortable life? What do I really want in this lifetime? What is this void I am trying to fill within myself that I can only soothe when I put friction in everything? Most of the time the void is as unnoticeable as a mosquito bite. Sometimes it widens and takes up my whole girth, so I feel like I can only run around and through the wind the empty hole makes a whistling sound, that I gleefully laugh at.
